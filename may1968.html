The Students Revolts of May 1968

Origins of the Revolt

In May 1968, students at Paris universities protested against outdated education, rigid authority, and capitalism. Their demands included university reforms, free speech, and more personal freedoms.

Nationwide Uprising

The protests spread across France, leading to massive strikes involving over 10 million workers. The country was nearly paralyzed. President Charles de Gaulle briefly fled the country before regaining control.

Impact on French Society

Though the protests were eventually quelled, they brought lasting change. Education reforms, worker rights, and modern attitudes toward authority were influenced by the movement. It remains a symbol of youth rebellion and societal transformation.
