French Colonisation and Decolonisation

Introduction

France built one of the largest colonial empires in history. Beginning in the 17th century, France established colonies in Africa, Asia, and the Americas. However, the mid-20th century saw a wave of decolonisation that ended the empire.

Colonial Era

French colonisation reached its peak during the 19th century, with territories spanning across North and West Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Caribbean.

Decolonisation

After World War II, France faced significant resistance to colonial rule. In regions like Indochina, Algeria, and sub-Saharan Africa, independence movements grew stronger, leading to the eventual loss of its colonies.
