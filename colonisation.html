French Colonisation and Decolonisation

The French Colonial Empire

Between the 17th and 20th centuries, France established colonies in Africa, Asia, the Caribbean, and the Pacific. This expansion aimed to exploit resources, spread culture, and compete with other European powers.

Struggles for Independence

After World War II, colonized nations began demanding independence. The most violent examples include the Algerian War (1954–1962) and the Indochina War (Vietnam). France faced resistance and changing global attitudes toward imperialism.

Post-Colonial France

Decolonisation reshaped France’s demographics, economy, and foreign relations. Migrants from former colonies now form part of France’s multicultural society, while debates over colonial history continue to influence politics and education.
