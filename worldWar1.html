France During World War 1

The Outbreak of War

France entered WWI as part of the Allies after Germany invaded Belgium. It faced massive destruction, particularly along the Western Front. Trenches, chemical warfare, and long stalemates defined the conflict.

Major Battles

The Battle of the Marne and the Battle of Verdun were particularly brutal. Verdun alone caused nearly 700,000 casualties and became a symbol of French endurance.

Aftermath

France suffered over 1.3 million deaths. The war devastated the countryside and economy but also strengthened national unity. The Treaty of Versailles in 1919 aimed to weaken Germany but sowed the seeds of WWII.
