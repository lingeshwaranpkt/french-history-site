French History Timeline

Explore major events in French history

52 BC - Battle of Alesia

Julius Caesar defeats Vercingetorix, marking the Roman conquest of Gaul. The battle showed Roman engineering and siege mastery.

1337-1453 - Hundred Years' War & Joan of Arc

A long war between France and England. Joan of Arc emerged as a national heroine, claiming divine guidance to lead France to victory.

16th Century - Renaissance & Humanism

The French Renaissance brought art, literature, and science. Humanism emphasized individual potential and classical learning.

1643-1715 - Louis XIV

Known as the Sun King, Louis XIV centralized power and built the Palace of Versailles, symbolizing absolute monarchy.

1815 - Napoleon & Battle of Waterloo

Napoleon's final defeat by the Seventh Coalition ended his rule and reshaped Europe's political map.

19th-20th Century - Colonisation & Decolonisation

France established a vast empire in Africa and Asia. Post-WW2 movements led to independence and the end of colonial rule.

1914-1918 - World War I

France fought on the Western Front in brutal trench warfare. The war left deep social and economic scars.

1939-1945 - World War II

Occupied by Nazi Germany, France saw resistance movements rise. The country was liberated in 1944.

May 1968 - Student Revolts

A cultural and political movement driven by students and workers. It led to major changes in society, education, and labor laws.
