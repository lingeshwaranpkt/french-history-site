French History Timeline

The Conquest of Gaul by Julius Caesar & the Battle of Alesia

The Battle of Alesia was a decisive battle between Julius Caesar's Roman legions and the forces of Vercingetorix, the leader of the Gallic tribes.

The Hundred Years War & Joan of Arc

The Hundred Years War was a series of conflicts waged from 1337 to 1453 between the Kingdom of England and the Kingdom of France. Joan of Arc played a pivotal role in the French victory.

The Renaissance & Humanism in France

The Renaissance in France was a period of cultural, artistic, and intellectual rebirth that began in the 15th century. Humanism played a key role in shaping French thought and culture during this time.

Louis XIV, The Sun King

Louis XIV, also known as the Sun King, ruled France for over 70 years, establishing absolute monarchy and expanding France's influence in Europe.

Napoleon and the Battle of Waterloo

Napoleon Bonaparte was a military leader and emperor who led France in a series of military campaigns. The Battle of Waterloo in 1815 marked his final defeat.

French Colonisation and Decolonisation

French colonisation involved establishing overseas colonies across the globe, while decolonisation in the 20th century saw the independence of many former colonies.

France During World War 1

World War 1 was a devastating conflict that lasted from 1914 to 1918, and France played a major role in the Allied victory.

France During World War 2

During World War 2, France was invaded by Nazi Germany, leading to the occupation of the country and the formation of the Vichy regime.

The Student Revolts of May 1968

In May 1968, a series of student protests and strikes spread across France, leading to social and political changes throughout the country.
