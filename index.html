Moments That Shaped France

A journey through key historical events in French history

52 BC – The Conquest of Gaul & Battle of Alesia

Julius Caesar led Roman forces to defeat Vercingetorix and the Gauls at the Battle of Alesia. This marked the beginning of Roman control over France.

1429 – The 100 Years’ War & Joan of Arc

Joan of Arc, a peasant girl, claimed divine guidance and led France to key victories, inspiring resistance against English rule during the Hundred Years' War.

16th Century – The Renaissance & Humanism

Humanism and art flourished during the French Renaissance. Thinkers like Montaigne and artists like Clouet helped shape cultural life in France.

1643–1715 – Louis XIV, The Sun King

Louis XIV built the Palace of Versailles and centralized power in the monarchy, defining the image of absolute royal authority in Europe.

1815 – Napoleon & The Battle of Waterloo

Napoleon's final defeat at Waterloo ended his reign and led to his exile. It marked a turning point in European power dynamics.

1880s–1960s – French Colonisation & Decolonisation

France built a vast overseas empire in Africa and Asia. The 20th century saw a wave of independence movements across the colonies.

1914–1918 – France During World War I

France was a major battlefield of WWI. The war caused massive destruction and loss, especially at battles like Verdun and the Somme.

1939–1945 – France During World War II

France fell to Nazi Germany in 1940 but resisted through the Free French Forces and the Resistance. It was liberated in 1944.

May 1968 – Student Revolts

A series of protests by students and workers shook French society, demanding reforms in education, rights, and democracy.
