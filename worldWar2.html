France During World War II

Invasion and Occupation

Germany invaded France in 1940, forcing it to surrender within six weeks. Northern France was occupied, while the south was governed by the Vichy regime â€” a puppet government collaborating with the Nazis.

Resistance and Liberation

The French Resistance fought through sabotage, intelligence, and aiding Allied forces. Charles de Gaulle led the Free French Forces from exile. France was finally liberated in 1944 after D-Day and Allied advances.

Post-War France

WWII reshaped France politically and socially. The Vichy legacy remained controversial, while the Resistance became a source of pride. The country focused on rebuilding and joined efforts for European unity.
