Louis XIV - The Sun King

Absolute Power and Centralization

Louis XIV reigned from 1643 to 1715, making him one of Europe’s longest-reigning monarchs. He ruled with absolute authority, concentrating all power under the monarchy. He famously said, “L'État, c'est moi” (I am the state).

The Palace of Versailles

To display his grandeur and control the nobility, Louis built the extravagant Palace of Versailles. It became the center of political life in France, with strict court etiquette and ceremonies that reinforced royal dominance.

Warfare and Decline

Though his reign brought cultural prestige and military might, it also led to costly wars and economic strain. By his death, France was powerful but deeply in debt, sowing seeds of discontent for future revolutions.
