Joan of Arc & The 100 Years' War

Introduction

Joan of Arc was a peasant girl who became a national heroine of France. She played a crucial role during the Hundred Years' War between France and England. Her faith and military leadership helped the French regain territories from English occupation.

Background

The Hundred Years' War (1337–1453) was a series of conflicts fought between the Kingdom of England and the Kingdom of France, each vying for control of territories and the French throne.

Joan's Role

At just 17, Joan convinced Charles VII to allow her to lead French troops. She had a series of military successes, including the lifting of the siege of Orléans, which helped turn the tide of the war in favor of the French.
